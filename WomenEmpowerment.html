    HOW CAN WE EMPOWER OURSELVES
    AND THE WOMEN AROUND US IN THIS ERA OF SOCIAL MEDIA?

    Regulation of social media platforms can target different aspects of the infrastructure, for example, the technology – by regulating algorithmic processing of content (e.g. on what basis targeting can be done) or the company – such as with antitrust legislation. The intersecting dynamics of gender, race, geography and sexuality also point to the importance of intersectional and interdisciplinary work to create a more equitable and inclusive foundation for online interactions.

    In addition to using social media to spread awareness, women can utilize social media to take action. There are numerous ways to accomplish this, including starting online petitions, staging protests, and raising funds for groups that strive to empower women. Social media can also be used to connect with other groups and individuals who are working on similar topics, which is an excellent approach to develop a support network.

    Promoting women’s empowerment online is complex and it encompasses various aspects of digital education, access to technology and gender equality.

    The aim is to bridge the digital gender gap and create an inclusive online environment that empowers women to utilize the internet with confidence.

    However, as everyone is aware, social media has also been utilized to harass and intimidate women online. These attacks specifically target women journalists and women in positions of influence. Research has repeatedly demonstrated that women face threats and attacks on the internet that are very different from those faced by men.

    Physical threats are directed at all genders, but those directed towards women are sexual in nature and are intended to establish dominance, frighten, and quiet. Because abusive remarks on social media get a lot of likes, some sexists have been inspired to come out of the shadows and preach prejudice.
